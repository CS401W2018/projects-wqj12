The entrepreneurial journey of Jack Ma

Jack Ma, born in Hangzhou, Zhejiang in 1964, is a Chinese entrepreneur and former chairman of the board of directors of Alibaba Group. He founded Taobao and Alipay and once became the richest man in Asia. Currently, he serves as an honorary professor at the School of Business and Economics of the University of Hong Kong.

Haibo Translation Agency
Haibo Translation Agency, which was maintained by reselling small commodities and organized the first English corner in Hangzhou. In 1995, he first came into contact with the Internet. After returning to China, he founded the "China Yellow Pages" website and earned 5 million RMB in three years.
Alibaba
In 1999, Jack Ma resigned from public office and founded Alibaba.com, focusing on e-commerce, especially B2B business. Taobao was founded in 2003 and Alipay was founded in 2004, quickly becoming a leader in the Chinese market
resignation as CEO of Alibaba
In May 2013, Jack Ma announced his resignation as CEO of Alibaba. In 2018, he visited Malaysia and said that he founded Alibaba in 1997 inspired by Mahathir's "Multimedia Super Corridor" concept.
